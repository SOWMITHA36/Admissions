Engineering

Engineering is the application of natural science, mathematics, and engineering design to solve technical problems, improve systems, and increase productivity and efficiency. Here are some different types of engineering offered by our university:

  • Civil engineering: Involves the design, construction, maintenance, and inspection of large civil infrastructure projects, such as roads, railways, bridges, tunnels, and dams.

    The Civil Engineering course duration at the postgraduate level is a two-year program, divided into four semesters. Some subjects include Theory of Elasticity, Industrial Structures, and Theory of Plates and Shells.

  • Mechanical engineering: Involves the design, development, and operation of mechanical systems and devices, including machinery, equipment, and components.

    Here are a few courses offered by our university for those pursuing further studies after mechanical engineering:
    - MTech in Mechanical Engineering
    - Mechatronics
    - Supply chain management
    - MBA
    - Piping design and engineering
    - Tool design
    - Nanotechnology
    - 3D modeling

  • Chemical engineering: Involves designing and troubleshooting processes for the production of chemicals, fuels, foods, pharmaceuticals, and biologicals.

    Master's degree courses in Chemical Engineering include:
    - Instrumental Chemical Analysis
    - Biotechnology and Chemical Engineering
    - Chemical and Biochemical Engineering
    - Engineering For Energy Transition
    - Advanced Chemical Engineering

  • Electrical engineering: Involves the design, testing, and construction of electrical and electronic devices, machinery, and systems.

    Postgraduate courses in electrical engineering include:
    - Master of Engineering in Electrical Engineering
    - ME Power Electronics and Drives
    - M. Tech in Power System Engineering
    - MSc Electrical and Electronic Engineering
